Nutraceutical Payment Processing: Solving FDA and Banking Challenges

By VirdisPay Team October 17, 2024 9 min read

The Nutraceutical Payment Crisis

If you sell dietary supplements, herbal products, or wellness items, you've likely encountered these payment processing challenges:

  • Payment processors labeling your business as "high-risk"
  • Being charged 4-7% in transaction fees
  • Sudden account terminations after mentioning "FDA" or "supplement"
  • Rolling reserves holding 10-15% of your revenue
  • Extensive compliance documentation requirements

Why Payment Processors Flag Nutraceutical Businesses

1. FDA Regulatory Concerns

Payment processors worry about:

  • Structure/function claims that might violate regulations
  • Products making health claims without FDA approval
  • Compliance with dietary supplement regulations
  • Liability if products are later recalled

2. High Chargeback Potential

The supplement industry sees elevated chargebacks due to:

  • Subscription model disputes
  • Customers claiming products "didn't work"
  • Trial-to-subscription conversions
  • Lengthy shipping times for international orders

3. Card Network Restrictions

Visa and Mastercard have specific rules about:

  • How supplements can be marketed
  • Claims that can be made
  • Acceptable product categories
  • Recurring billing disclosures

The Cost of Traditional Payment Processing

Typical Fees for Supplement Businesses:

  • Transaction fees: 4-7% (vs. 2-3% for low-risk merchants)
  • Monthly gateway fees: $25-$50
  • PCI compliance fees: $99-$299/year
  • Chargeback fees: $25-$100 per dispute
  • Rolling reserve: 10-15% held for 6 months

Example: $100,000 in monthly sales

  • Processing fees (5%): $5,000
  • Monthly fees: $50
  • Chargeback fees (2% rate): $500
  • Total monthly cost: $5,550
