Hemp Industry Banking Challenges in 2024: How Crypto Payments Provide Solutions

By VirdisPay Team January 20, 2024 7 min read

Six years after the 2018 Farm Bill legalized hemp at the federal level, the hemp industry continues to face significant banking challenges that threaten business growth and industry stability. Despite hemp being federally legal, financial institutions remain hesitant to serve hemp businesses, creating a complex landscape that crypto payments are uniquely positioned to solve.

This comprehensive analysis explores the current state of hemp industry banking, the persistent challenges facing hemp businesses, and how cryptocurrency payments offer a revolutionary solution to these long-standing problems.

The Hemp Banking Landscape in 2024

Current Statistics

Recent industry surveys reveal the ongoing banking crisis in the hemp sector:

  • Account Access: Only 38% of hemp businesses have access to traditional banking services
  • Account Closures: 67% of hemp businesses report having accounts closed without warning
  • Fee Structure: Hemp businesses pay 3-5x higher banking fees than traditional businesses
  • Processing Limits: 89% of hemp businesses face transaction volume restrictions

Federal vs State Banking Guidance

The disconnect between federal and state guidance creates confusion for financial institutions:

  • Federal Level: 2018 Farm Bill legalized hemp, but banking guidance remains unclear
  • FinCEN Guidance: 2019 guidance provided some clarity but left many questions unanswered
  • State Variations: Each state has different hemp regulations and banking requirements
  • Bank Interpretation: Individual banks interpret regulations differently
"The hemp industry generates over $8 billion annually in the US, yet most businesses operate in a banking gray area. This creates an enormous opportunity for cryptocurrency payments to provide the financial infrastructure the industry desperately needs."

Specific Banking Challenges Facing Hemp Businesses

1. Account Opening Difficulties

Hemp businesses face numerous obstacles when trying to open bank accounts:

  • Enhanced Due Diligence: Extensive documentation requirements beyond normal business accounts
  • Industry Categorization: Many banks lump hemp with cannabis, applying stricter regulations
  • Geographic Restrictions: Some banks refuse to serve hemp businesses in certain states
  • Business Type Limitations: Certain hemp business models face additional restrictions

2. Account Maintenance Challenges

Even after opening accounts, hemp businesses face ongoing challenges:

  • High Maintenance Fees: Monthly fees ranging from $200-1,000
  • Reserve Requirements: 10-25% of monthly volume held in reserve
  • Transaction Monitoring: Increased scrutiny on all transactions
  • Regular Reviews: Quarterly or monthly account reviews with potential for closure

3. Payment Processing Limitations

  • High Processing Fees: 5-8% transaction fees compared to 2.9% for traditional businesses
  • Volume Restrictions: Monthly processing limits that hinder business growth
  • Chargeback Risks: Higher chargeback rates due to customer confusion
  • International Restrictions: Limited ability to process international payments

4. Regulatory Compliance Burden

  • Documentation Requirements: Extensive paperwork for every transaction
  • Reporting Obligations: Regular reports to financial institutions
  • Audit Requirements: Frequent audits and compliance reviews
  • Legal Costs: Significant legal expenses for compliance

Why Traditional Banking Fails Hemp Businesses

Risk Aversion

Financial institutions remain risk-averse despite hemp's legal status:

  • Regulatory Uncertainty: Unclear federal guidance creates perceived risks
  • Reputation Concerns: Fear of negative publicity from serving hemp businesses
  • Compliance Costs: High costs of maintaining hemp-specific compliance programs
  • Insurance Limitations: Limited insurance coverage for hemp-related banking activities

Systemic Issues

  • Outdated Systems: Banking infrastructure not designed for hemp businesses
  • Training Gaps: Bank staff lack knowledge about hemp industry specifics
  • Policy Inconsistencies: Different policies across branches and regions
  • Slow Adaptation: Banks slow to adapt to changing hemp regulations

Regional Variations in Hemp Banking

Hemp-Friendly Regions

Some regions have more supportive banking environments:

  • Kentucky: Strong hemp banking infrastructure due to early legalization
  • Colorado: Established hemp industry with supportive financial institutions
  • Oregon: Progressive hemp policies with banking support
  • North Carolina: Growing hemp industry with increasing banking access

Challenging Regions

  • California: Despite hemp legalization, banking remains difficult
  • Texas: Conservative banking environment with limited hemp support
  • Florida: Mixed banking policies across different institutions
  • New York: Complex regulations create banking challenges
