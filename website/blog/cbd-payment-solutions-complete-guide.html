CBD Payment Solutions: Complete Guide for CBD Businesses in 2024

By VirdisPay Team January 18, 2024 10 min read

The CBD industry has exploded in recent years, with the global market expected to reach $47.22 billion by 2028. However, despite this massive growth, CBD businesses continue to face significant payment processing challenges that threaten their ability to scale and compete effectively.

From banking restrictions to high processing fees, CBD companies need innovative payment solutions that can handle their unique business model while maintaining compliance with evolving regulations. This comprehensive guide explores the payment landscape for CBD businesses and how cryptocurrency payments are revolutionizing the industry.

The CBD Payment Crisis: Why Traditional Banking Fails CBD Businesses

Despite the 2018 Farm Bill legalizing hemp-derived CBD at the federal level, CBD businesses still face numerous payment processing challenges:

Banking Challenges

  • Account Closures: 73% of CBD businesses report having their bank accounts closed unexpectedly
  • High-Risk Classification: Most payment processors categorize CBD as "high-risk," resulting in exorbitant fees
  • Limited Banking Options: Only 12% of financial institutions actively serve CBD businesses
  • Inconsistent Policies: Bank policies vary widely, even within the same institution

Payment Processing Issues

  • High Processing Fees: 5-8% transaction fees compared to 2.9% for traditional businesses
  • Chargeback Risks: Higher chargeback rates due to customer confusion about CBD products
  • Account Holds: Reserve requirements of 10-25% of monthly volume
  • Sudden Terminations: Processors can terminate accounts without notice
"The CBD industry generates over $4 billion annually in the US alone, yet most businesses struggle to find reliable, affordable payment processing. Cryptocurrency payments offer a legitimate alternative that bypasses traditional banking restrictions entirely."

Understanding CBD Payment Regulations

Federal Level (USA)

The regulatory landscape for CBD payments involves multiple federal agencies:

  • FDA Regulations: CBD products must comply with FDA guidelines for dietary supplements or cosmetics
  • FTC Oversight: Marketing claims must be substantiated and not misleading
  • USDA Hemp Program: Hemp cultivation must meet USDA standards
  • Banking Guidance: FinCEN provides guidance for financial institutions serving hemp businesses

State-Level Variations

State regulations vary significantly, affecting payment processing:

  • Hemp-Friendly States: Kentucky, Colorado, Oregon have supportive banking relationships
  • Restrictive States: Some states still have complex hemp regulations
  • Licensing Requirements: Many states require specific licenses for CBD businesses
  • Testing Requirements: State-specific testing and compliance standards

International Considerations

  • EU Novel Food Regulations: CBD products must be authorized as novel foods
  • UK Regulations: CBD products must be registered with the FSA
  • Cross-Border Payments: International CBD sales face additional compliance requirements

Traditional Payment Solutions for CBD Businesses

High-Risk Payment Processors

Several processors specialize in high-risk industries, including CBD:

  • Square: Recently expanded CBD support with specific requirements
  • PayPal: Limited CBD support with strict compliance requirements
  • Stripe: Does not currently support CBD businesses
  • Specialized Processors: Companies like PaymentCloud and Easy Pay Direct

Challenges with Traditional Solutions

Cost Breakdown for Traditional CBD Payment Processing:

  • Transaction Fees: 5-8% per transaction
  • Monthly Fees: $50-200 per month
  • Reserve Requirements: 10-25% of monthly volume held for 180 days
  • Chargeback Fees: $15-25 per chargeback
  • Setup Fees: $200-500 one-time setup
  • Termination Fees: Up to $500 if account is closed
